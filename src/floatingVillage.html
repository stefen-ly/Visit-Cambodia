Floating Village

Highlights

  • Kompong Phluk – Stilted houses and flooded mangrove forests
  • Kompong Khleang – The largest and least touristy floating village
  • Chong Kneas – Closest to Siem Reap with vibrant boat life
  • Floating schools, markets, and pagodas on Tonlé Sap Lake
  • Sunset cruises and birdwatching experiences

The Floating Villages of Siem Reap offer a rare and immersive glimpse into life on Cambodia’s mighty Tonlé Sap Lake. These water-based communities live entirely in harmony with the changing tides and seasons of Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake.

One of the most well-known villages is Kompong Phluk, where homes rise on towering stilts above seasonal floodwaters. During the wet season, the entire area transforms into a picturesque waterworld, with boat traffic replacing roads. Visitors can cruise through the flooded mangrove forests that surround the village.

Kompong Khleang is the largest of the floating villages and offers a more authentic, less commercialized experience. With its sprawling waterways, floating schools, and bustling fish markets, it provides deep insight into the daily rhythms of Cambodian lake life.

Chong Kneas, located just a short drive from Siem Reap town, is the most accessible. Though more touristy, it introduces travelers to the concept of floating living — including houseboats, shops, and even floating pagodas.

The Tonlé Sap Lake itself is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and home to a rich ecosystem. Many tours include stops at local bird sanctuaries, where you can see rare waterfowl and migratory species. It’s a must-visit for nature lovers and photographers.

A visit to the Floating Village is not just a scenic experience, but also a cultural one. Tourists can interact with locals, visit floating schools, or learn how communities adapt to life on water, including fishing, farming, and education.

For the best experience, plan a visit during the rainy season (June to November), when the water levels are high and the villages appear to float above the lake. Sunset boat tours are especially magical, offering golden reflections across the water and silhouettes of stilt houses and trees.

Exploring the Floating Villages of Siem Reap gives you a deeper appreciation for Cambodia's cultural diversity and natural resilience. It’s a peaceful, powerful contrast to the grandeur of Angkor and a beautiful way to connect with the heartbeat of local life.
