Home > Destinations > Siemreap

Siem Reap is a charming province in northwestern Cambodia, known as the gateway to the world-famous Angkor Wat temple complex. The region is mainly flat and surrounded by rice fields, small villages, and traditional Khmer countryside. The Siem Reap River runs through the town, adding to its scenic charm. Key attractions include the Angkor Archaeological Park, which houses majestic temples like Angkor Wat, Bayon, and Ta Prohm. Other interesting places to visit are the Angkor National Museum, the Cambodian Cultural Village, and the floating villages on Tonle Sap Lake. If you enjoy shopping and local culture, Siem Reap offers bustling markets such as the Old Market (Phsar Chas), Angkor Night Market, and Made in Cambodia Market. You can also enjoy boat tours on Tonle Sap Lake and traditional Apsara dance performances in the evening.

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is the most iconic and awe-inspiring temple complex in Cambodia and one of the largest religious monuments in the world. Located just outside of Siem Reap, it was originally built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II as a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Vishnu. Later, it transformed into a Buddhist temple, which it remains today.
