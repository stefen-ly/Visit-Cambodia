Kirirom National Park

Highlights

  • Refreshing pine forest trails with panoramic views
  • Chambok ecotourism site with waterfalls and rural experiences
  • Mountain biking and camping opportunities
  • Cooler temperatures year-round – perfect escape from city heat
  • Less crowded than major tourist destinations

Located in Kampong Speu province, Kirirom National Park is Cambodia’s first officially designated national park, offering a serene natural retreat from the urban rush of Phnom Penh. It's well known for its pine forests, scenic mountain vistas, and refreshing climate.

The name “Kirirom” means “Happy Mountain” in Khmer, and it lives up to that name with peaceful picnic areas, walking trails, and fresh air. Unlike the typical tropical lowlands, Kirirom features highland forest ecosystems with tall pines — unique for Cambodia.

One of the main attractions is the Chambok Community-Based Ecotourism site. Here, visitors can explore waterfalls, hike through the forest, and learn from local villagers about sustainable living practices.

Adventurous travelers enjoy hiking and mountain biking across the park's trails, some of which lead to remote viewpoints and hidden waterfalls. Birdwatching is also popular, with various native species inhabiting the area.

For those seeking to stay overnight, Kirirom offers both camping grounds and eco-resorts, making it ideal for a weekend getaway. Visitors can enjoy starry nights, outdoor fires, and the tranquil sounds of nature.

The best time to visit is during the cooler season from November to February, though it's open year-round. Just a two-hour drive from Phnom Penh, Kirirom is easily accessible and remains one of Cambodia’s best-kept nature secrets.

Whether you’re a hiker, a photographer, or just someone needing a breath of fresh air, Kirirom National Park provides a perfect blend of scenic beauty, cultural immersion, and peaceful escape.
